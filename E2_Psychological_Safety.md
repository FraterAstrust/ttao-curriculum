### What This Document Is

The TTAO curriculum includes guided imaginal and inner work practices — principally the Desoille Directed Waking Dream sequences, which involve deliberate contact with subconscious material through a structured, meditative descent. These practices carry inherent psychological risk if approached without appropriate awareness, preparation, or external professional support.

**This document provides educational information about these risks and guidance for self-management. It is not a guarantee of safety, nor does it imply that the Academy undertakes any responsibility for mitigating these risks beyond providing educational content.**

### The First Year as Preparation

Year One's inner work is specifically designed to build self-knowledge and inner stability before the more intensive practices are assigned. Do not shortcut the foundation. Do not advance to Month 6 inner work if the meditation practice has not been consistently established.

### Contraindications — When Not to Proceed with Deep Descent Work

**Disclaimer:** The following contraindications are provided for educational awareness only. Students are SOLELY responsible for self-assessing their suitability for deep inner work and for consulting with a qualified medical or mental health professional if any of these conditions apply.

Do not engage in the descent practices if any of the following apply:

- You are currently experiencing **acute psychosis, mania, or severe dissociation**
- You have experienced a **significant traumatic event within the last six months** and have not yet begun processing it with professional support
- You have a diagnosed **dissociative disorder (DID or OSDD)** without a currently active treating therapist
- You have **untreated panic disorder** that has triggered during meditation or closed-eye visualisation
- You are currently in an **acute mental health crisis** of any kind
- You are experiencing **active suicidal ideation**

### Mandatory Cessation Thresholds — When a Guide MUST Stop Instruction

The following conditions require a Guide to **immediately and without exception cease all inner work instruction** and direct the student to external professional resources. These are not judgment calls. They are administrative thresholds.

**A Guide must cease inner work instruction when a student reports or demonstrates:**

1. **Active suicidal ideation** — any expression of intent or plan to end their life
2. **Acute psychotic symptoms** — loss of contact with reality, hallucinations, delusions that are not part of the waking dream practice
3. **Inability to distinguish between waking dream imagery and physical reality** beyond the session itself
4. **Self-harm behaviour** within the reporting period
5. **Prolonged dissociation** — inability to return to ordinary grounded awareness more than 24 hours after a session
6. **Expressed intent to harm another person**

Upon reaching any of the above thresholds, a Guide:
- **Stops all inner work instruction immediately**
- Provides the student with appropriate crisis resources (see Crisis-Response SOP)
- Notifies the Headmaster
- Does **not** attempt to counsel, process, or therapeutically support the student — this is beyond the Guide's scope
- Documents the interaction per the Crisis-Response SOP

**A Guide does not determine whether a student is psychologically healthy or unwell.** A Guide observes the student's reported experience, applies the thresholds above as administrative rules, and refers to external professionals when thresholds are met. No clinical judgment is exercised by Guide or Headmaster.

### Grounding After a Difficult Session

If a session produces distress or difficulty returning to ordinary awareness:

1. Stop the practice immediately.
2. Ground physically: press feet on the floor, feel the chair, look at three objects and name them aloud.
3. Breathe: three slow deliberate breaths with full exhale.
4. Do something ordinary: drink water, wash hands, go outside briefly.
5. Write briefly in the journal: a factual account of what happened.
6. Do not attempt another session the same day.

### The Referral Pathway

**Disclaimer:** Steps below are educational guidance only. TTAO Inner Academy does NOT provide referrals, endorse specific practitioners, or assume any responsibility for the accessibility, quality, or outcome of external mental health services. Students are solely responsible for identifying, vetting, and engaging their own mental health professionals.

1. If you have a therapist, inform them of the practice and ask for support.
2. If you do not have a therapist and distress is significant, seek a trauma-informed practitioner via your GP or professional association directory.
3. If you are in crisis, contact your local emergency mental health service or crisis line immediately:
   - **US:** 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, call or text)
   - **Oregon specifically:** Lines for Life 1-800-273-8255; Crisis Text Line: text "Oregon" to 741741
   - **International:** findahelpline.com

### Supplementary Self-Regulation Tools

EFT Tapping and the Sedona Method are supplementary tools for working with identified emotional blockages. They are **not** replacements for professional support when deeper destabilisation occurs. Do not apply these to acute trauma, crisis states, or dissociative episodes.
