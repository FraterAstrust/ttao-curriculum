## Section 4: Psychological Screening & Contraindications

### 4.1 Purpose of This Section

The TTAO Inner Academy curriculum involves deep inner work, including shadow integration, visualization, and engagement with unconscious material. This work can be profoundly transformative, but it is not appropriate for everyone at all times.

**This section is designed solely for the academy's internal risk management and to inform you of the types of psychological considerations relevant to this educational program.** It allows us to understand your self-reported background and make informed decisions about your educational pathway within the academy.

**It is NOT a diagnostic tool, psychological assessment, or a substitute for professional mental health evaluation or treatment.**

This section asks about psychological and medical factors that *you report* and which *may indicate a need for modified participation, additional external support, or temporary pause in enrollment*. **Your answers will not automatically disqualify you.** They help the Headmaster make informed decisions about how to support you safely *within the educational framework*.

**By completing this section, you acknowledge and reaffirm your sole responsibility for managing your own mental health and seeking appropriate professional care when needed.**

## Document Retention & Privacy Policy

All intake forms are retained securely by the Headmaster for the duration of the student's enrollment and for **7 years** following withdrawal or completion, in accordance with standard educational record-keeping practices.

Intake information is shared only with the student's assigned Guide(s) on a need-to-know basis. It is never shared with other students or external parties without the student's explicit written consent, except as required by law (e.g., mandatory reporting of child abuse, imminent threat to self or others).

Students may request a copy of their intake form at any time by contacting the Headmaster.
