# TTAO Inner Academy

## Safety Document E.5 — Community Conduct, Confidentiality & Ethics Agreement

*This document is signed by every student at enrollment. It governs all participation in TTAO — including the online academy, dashboard activity, quarterly gatherings, guide sessions, and any communication with fellow students or academy staff.*

---

### Preamble

The TTAO Inner Academy is founded on the oldest requirement of the alchemical tradition: sincerity. The practitioner who enters this Work insincerely — performing the outer forms while concealing or misrepresenting their actual state — does not merely waste their own time. They undermine the integrity of the circle of which they are a part.

Every student who enrolls in TTAO is asked to make one foundational commitment, from which all others in this document flow: **absolute honesty in engagement with the Work**. This means honesty with oneself, in the laboratory journal and the daily record. It means honesty with one's guide and with the headmaster. It means honesty with fellow students in the context of gatherings and shared work.

This agreement formalises what that commitment requires in practice.

---

### Part I — Personal Conduct and Honesty

**1.1 Honesty in Self-Reporting**
The student commits to honest self-reporting in all academy contexts: the dashboard, the laboratory journal, the quarterly gatherings, guide sessions, and any other form of communication with academy staff or fellow students. Exaggeration of results, fabrication of laboratory records, misrepresentation of inner work progress, or performance of sincerity that does not reflect the student's actual state are violations of this agreement and grounds for review.

Self-deception is a more common danger than deliberate fraud, so be honest with yourself at all times.

**1.2 The Declaration of Intent**
The Declaration of Intent written in Year One Month 1 is a sincere statement of purpose. The student agrees to return to it periodically throughout the curriculum and to approach the headmaster or guide if their purpose has significantly shifted — including if doubt, disillusionment, or a change in life circumstances has altered their engagement with the Work.

**1.3 Honesty About Capacity**
If a student is unable to continue their work for any reason — health, circumstance, family, employment, or otherwise — they agree to notify their guide or the headmaster rather than simply withdrawing silently. The academy is competency-paced and takes long interruptions in stride. What it cannot accommodate is unexplained disappearance, which leaves preparatory work without oversight and fellow students without the circle member they expected.

---

### Part II — Confidentiality

**2.1 What Is Shared in Gatherings Stays in Gatherings**
Quarterly Tier Gatherings may involve the sharing of personal reflections, inner work experiences, struggles, and genuine self-examination. What a fellow student shares in this context is held in confidence by all present. It is not discussed outside the gathering, not shared with third parties, and not referenced in any public forum.

**2.2 Guide Sessions**
Content shared in personal guide sessions is confidential between the student and their guide. Guides do not share session content with other students. They may consult with the headmaster on matters affecting a student's safety or wellbeing, with discretion. Students should understand that confidentiality in guide sessions does not extend to situations involving imminent risk of harm to the student or another person — in those cases, the headmaster will be notified and appropriate support will be sought.

**2.3 Dashboard and Shared Journal**
Students who post on the shared dashboard are posting within the enrolled student community. Posts visible to the community should be written with the understanding that all current students and guides may read them. Private journal entries kept personally do not enter the shared space without the student's choice.

**2.4 Proprietary Curriculum Materials**
Course materials, source adaptations, proprietary inner work content, and structured lesson materials produced by TTAO are for the use of enrolled students only. They are not to be shared outside the academy community, distributed publicly, or reproduced for use in other courses or teaching contexts without the explicit written permission of the headmaster. This obligation continues after a student has completed or left the program.

---

### Part III — Community Care and Conflict

**3.1 Engagement with Fellow Students**
Students engage with one another with patience, care, and respect for the path each is walking. The quarterly gathering is a circle, not a competition. The Work is not a basis for hierarchy among students — only for different stages of a shared path. Students are expected to listen genuinely, to refrain from dismissing or diminishing another's experience, and to bring the same non-interfering attention to a fellow student's sharing that they bring to their own flask.

**3.2 Differences of Understanding and Practice**
Students will inevitably hold different interpretations of the alchemical tradition, different approaches to inner work, and different relationships to the symbolic frameworks introduced in the curriculum. These differences are welcome and generative. The grounds for concern are not difference of view but failure of care: persistent condescension, the use of another's vulnerability against them, or the attempt to dominate group dynamics in the gathering context.

**3.3 Reporting Concerns**
If a student believes their own boundaries have been violated, or witnesses conduct that violates this agreement, they bring it to the headmaster directly. A prompt response is guaranteed. The student raising the concern will not be penalised for raising it.

**3.4 Resolution Process**
The first response to a reported conduct concern is a scheduled conversation between the headmaster and the student whose conduct is in question. Most concerns can be resolved at this stage — the alchemical tradition rightly expects that a sincere practitioner, when their conduct is named honestly, will recognise the issue before it needs to be named twice. If the conduct continues after a counseling session, suspension pending review may follow.

**3.5 Removal**
Removal from the academy is reserved for situations involving sustained dishonesty, persistent violation of this agreement after intervention, conduct that materially harms another student, or sharing of proprietary materials in violation of 2.4. Removal is an act of last resort, not a routine disciplinary tool.

---

### Part IV — NDA and Liability Release

**4.1 Non-Disclosure**
By enrolling in TTAO, the student agrees not to disclose, reproduce, or distribute proprietary curriculum content, source adaptations, or protected intellectual property of the TTAO Inner Academy. This agreement applies during enrollment and after departure from the academy, with no expiration.

**4.2 Assumption of Risk**
The student acknowledges that laboratory alchemy involves inherent physical risks — heat, flammable materials, glass, and in Year Three, toxic compounds — and that inner work involves inherent psychological risks. The student has read and understood the relevant safety documents (E.1, E.2, E.3, E.4) and accepts full personal responsibility for the implementation of safety precautions in their own workspace and for their own mental health decisions.

**4.3 Limitation of Academy Liability**
TTAO provides instruction, guidance, source materials, and community support. It does not control the physical workspace, equipment, materials, or personal choices of the student. TTAO, the headmaster, and all guides are not liable for harm resulting from a student's own engagement in laboratory work or inner practices, whether or not that harm was foreseeable.

The student is advised that this limitation of liability does not supersede any mandatory legal obligations under applicable law in their jurisdiction.

**4.4 No Guarantee of Outcome**
TTAO makes no guarantee that any specific alchemical result — including the Plant Stone or any preparation at any tier — will be achieved through participation in the curriculum. The Work proceeds at its own pace and in accordance with the practitioner's sincerity, attention, and capacity. The academy provides the most serious and well-grounded framework it can offer. The Work itself answers only to its own nature.
