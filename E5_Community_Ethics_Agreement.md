### Preamble

The TTAO Inner Academy is founded on the oldest requirement of the alchemical tradition: sincerity. The practitioner who enters this Work insincerely — performing the outer forms while concealing or misrepresenting their actual state — does not merely waste their own time. They undermine the integrity of the circle of which they are a part.

Every student who enrolls in TTAO is asked to make one foundational commitment, from which all others in this document flow: **absolute honesty in engagement with the Work, within the educational context of the academy**. This means honesty with oneself, in the laboratory journal and the daily record. It means honesty with one's guide and with the headmaster. It means honesty with fellow students in the context of gatherings and shared work.

This agreement formalises what that commitment requires in practice. **The academy's enforcement of these ethical guidelines is for the purpose of maintaining educational integrity and a functional learning environment, and does not constitute moral judgment or legal arbitration.**

---

### Part I — Personal Conduct and Honesty

**1.1 Honesty in Self-Reporting**
The student commits to honest self-reporting in all academy contexts: the dashboard, the laboratory journal, the quarterly gatherings, guide sessions, and any other form of communication with academy staff or fellow students. Exaggeration of results, fabrication of laboratory records, misrepresentation of inner work progress, or performance of sincerity that does not reflect the student's actual state are violations of this agreement and grounds for review.

Self-deception is a more common danger than deliberate fraud, so be honest with yourself at all times.

**1.2 The Declaration of Intent**
The Declaration of Intent written in Year One Month 1 is a sincere statement of purpose. The student agrees to return to it periodically throughout the curriculum and to approach the headmaster or guide if their purpose has significantly shifted — including if doubt, disillusionment, or a change in life circumstances has altered their engagement with the Work.

**1.3 Honesty About Capacity**
If a student is unable to continue their work for any reason — health, circumstance, family, employment, or otherwise — they agree to notify their guide or the headmaster rather than simply withdrawing silently. The academy is competency-paced and takes long interruptions in stride. What it cannot accommodate is unexplained disappearance, which leaves preparatory work without oversight and fellow students without the circle member they expected.

### Part II — Confidentiality

**Disclaimer:** While the academy encourages a culture of mutual respect and discretion, **TTAO Inner Academy cannot guarantee the confidentiality of any information shared by students within the community, including in gatherings, shared forums, or direct communications with other students.** Students are solely responsible for exercising their own judgment about what personal or sensitive information they choose to share, and with whom. The academy disclaims all liability for any breaches of confidentiality by other students or third parties.

**2.1 What Is Shared in Gatherings Stays in Gatherings (Ethical Expectation, Not Legal Guarantee)**
Quarterly Tier Gatherings may involve the sharing of personal reflections, inner work experiences, struggles, and genuine self-examination. What a fellow student shares in this context is ethically expected to be held in confidence by all present. However, **the academy cannot legally enforce this expectation among students and is not liable for any breaches of confidentiality by students.**

**2.2 Guide Sessions**
Content shared in personal guide sessions is confidential between the student and their guide, *subject to legal and ethical obligations*, including those outlined in the Crisis-Response SOP. Guides do not share session content with other students. They *may*, in their discretion, consult with the headmaster on matters affecting a student's safety or wellbeing, with discretion. Students should understand that confidentiality in guide sessions does not extend to situations involving imminent risk of harm to the student or another person, or where legally mandated — in those cases, the headmaster *may be* notified and appropriate external support *may be* sought.

**2.3 Dashboard and Shared Journal**
Students who post on the shared dashboard are posting within the enrolled student community. Posts visible to the community should be written with the understanding that all current students and guides may read them. Private journal entries kept personally do not enter the shared space without the student's choice. **The academy is not responsible for the content posted by students or for how other students may use or interpret such content.**

**2.4 Proprietary Curriculum Materials**
Course materials, source adaptations, proprietary inner work content, and structured lesson materials produced by TTAO are for the use of enrolled students only. They are not to be shared outside the academy community, distributed publicly, or reproduced for use in other courses or teaching contexts without the explicit written permission of the headmaster. This obligation continues after a student has completed or left the program. **Any unauthorized sharing of materials by a student is a violation of this agreement and may lead to immediate access revocation, and the academy disclaims any liability for such unauthorized sharing by students.**

### Part III — Community Care and Conflict

**Disclaimer:** TTAO Inner Academy fosters an educational community and provides administrative oversight. **However, the academy is NOT responsible for mediating interpersonal conflicts, guaranteeing social harmony, or resolving all disputes between students.** Students are expected to engage respectfully but remain solely responsible for their interactions. The academy disclaims all liability for emotional distress, damages, or losses arising from conflicts, disagreements, or the conduct of other students.

**3.1 Engagement with Fellow Students**
Students are expected to engage with one another with patience, care, and respect for the path each is walking, *within the spirit of an educational community*. The quarterly gathering is a circle, not a competition. The Work is not a basis for hierarchy among students — only for different stages of a shared path. Students are expected to listen genuinely, to refrain from dismissing or diminishing another's experience, and to bring the same non-interfering attention to a fellow student's sharing that they bring to their own flask. **The academy encourages respectful interaction but does not guarantee it and is not liable for individual student conduct.**

**3.2 Differences of Understanding and Practice**
Students will inevitably hold different interpretations of the alchemical tradition, different approaches to inner work, and different relationships to the symbolic frameworks introduced in the curriculum. These differences are welcome and generative. The grounds for administrative concern are not difference of view but clear violations of respectful conduct: persistent condescension, harassment, the use of another's vulnerability against them, or the attempt to dominate group dynamics in the gathering context.

**3.3 Reporting Concerns**
If a student believes their own boundaries have been violated, or witnesses conduct that violates this agreement, they are encouraged to bring it to the headmaster directly. A prompt administrative review will be guaranteed. The student raising the concern will not be penalised for raising it. **The academy's response will be administrative, focused on maintaining the educational environment, and does not imply a legal obligation to resolve the dispute or guarantee satisfaction.**

**3.4 Resolution Process**
The first administrative response to a reported conduct concern is a scheduled conversation between the headmaster and the student whose conduct is in question. Most concerns can be resolved at this stage — the alchemical tradition rightly expects that a sincere practitioner, when their conduct is named honestly, will recognise the issue before it needs to be named twice. If the conduct continues after a counseling session, suspension pending review *for administrative purposes* may follow.

**3.5 Administrative Removal**
Administrative removal from the academy is reserved for situations involving sustained dishonesty, persistent violation of this agreement after intervention, conduct that materially harms another student or the educational environment, or sharing of proprietary materials in violation of 2.4. Removal is an act of last resort, enacted *solely to preserve the integrity of the educational program*, not as a legal or punitive measure.
