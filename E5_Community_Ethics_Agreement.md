# TTAO Inner Academy

## Safety Document E.5 — Community Ethics Agreement

---

# IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER AND RELEASE OF LIABILITY

**This document establishes the ethical expectations governing participation in the TTAO Inner Academy community — its forums, Gatherings, Guide relationships, and any informal student-to-student contact facilitated or known to the Academy.**

**TTAO Inner Academy is an educational institution operating as an online remote programme. The Academy offers instruction in alchemical history, philosophy, and practical method. The Academy's staff — Headmaster, Guides, and any other affiliates — are educators only. They are not licensed medical or psychological professionals, legal advisors, financial counsellors, or life coaches.**

**The community structures of this Academy (Gatherings, Guide contacts, peer forums where offered) are educational support structures. They are not therapeutic groups, support groups, pastoral care, or licensed counselling services.**

**TTAO Inner Academy explicitly DISCLAIMS ALL LIABILITY for any harm, loss, relational conflict, psychological difficulty, or other adverse outcome arising from participation in any community structure of this Academy. This release is governed by the laws of the State of Oregon.**

### Preamble

Alchemical work is individual work. The tradition teaches that the operator works alone. At the same time, no student of the Great Work lives in complete isolation: there is a community of inquiry, of shared study, and of mutual encouragement. TTAO Inner Academy provides community structures to support this dimension of the Work.

The following agreements govern how students engage within those structures. They are not bureaucratic rules. They are a description of the character this community aspires to maintain, and the minimum ethical baseline without which the community cannot function safely.

---

### Part One — Conduct Within the Community

**Honesty:** Students are expected to report on their practical work honestly, including failures, confusions, and difficulties. The alchemical tradition takes seriously the distinction between genuine results and what the imagination supplies to fill the gap. Exaggeration of results, fabrication of experiences, or performance of attainment where none exists undermines the shared inquiry.

**Respect:** Students engage with each other and with Guides with basic courtesy. Philosophical disagreement is welcome. Contempt, condescension, manipulation, or coercion are not.

**Confidentiality within community structures:** What is shared in Gatherings or in Guide conversations is shared in confidence. Students do not repeat the personal disclosures of other students outside the community. This confidentiality does not extend to disclosures that suggest imminent risk of harm to self or others; the Guide will exercise judgment in such cases (see E.2 for escalation protocols).

**No unsolicited advice:** Students do not offer medical, psychological, legal, or financial advice to other students or to their Guides, solicited or otherwise. The appropriate response when a fellow student appears to be struggling is to encourage them toward appropriate professional support, not to attempt to provide it.

**No proselytising:** The alchemical tradition encompasses a wide range of philosophical and spiritual orientations. Students do not pressure others to adopt their views, practices, or interpretations.

---

### Part Two — The Guide Relationship

Guides are assigned at the beginning of Year Two to support students through Years Two and Three. The Guide relationship is an **educational facilitation relationship**, not a therapeutic, mentoring, pastoral, or personal relationship.

The Guide's role is limited to:
- Supporting the student's engagement with the curriculum
- Reviewing alchemical records and journal reflections for educational purposes
- Coordinating safety assessment processes (quiz scheduling, document submission)
- Signposting students toward appropriate professional resources when personal difficulties arise

**Guides do not:**
- Assess or advise on psychological health or wellbeing
- Advise on medical matters or the use of any preparation
- Serve as personal mentors, spiritual directors, or therapists
- Provide emergency psychological support (they refer to established crisis resources)

The Guide relationship is a structured professional relationship with the above-defined scope. Students who seek to expand the Guide relationship beyond this scope — whether toward personal advice, friendship, therapeutic disclosure, or informal preparation consultation — will be redirected to appropriate resources.

**Guides do not have authority to override Academy policies, modify safety requirements, or grant exceptions to any rule in any Safety Document.**

---

### Part Three — Sharing of Preparations

This section restates the prohibition contained in Safety Document E.4 within the community context.

**No student may share, give, sell, barter, or otherwise transfer any self-produced preparation to any other student, Guide, or Academy affiliate, whether in person, by post, or by any other means.**

This prohibition applies equally within community contexts. No community event, Gathering, peer connection, or Guide relationship creates an exception to this rule.

**No Guide, Headmaster, or Academy affiliate will accept or receive any self-produced preparation from a student.** Any offer to do so should be declined and reported to the Headmaster.

Violation of this prohibition by a student is grounds for immediate administrative removal from the Academy.

---

### Part Four — Transmission, Teaching, and External Sharing

As students advance through Year Three, questions naturally arise about how to engage with the broader alchemical community and tradition. The curriculum addresses this directly in Year Three Month 11.

The following principles govern students' use of their Academy education externally:

**Attribution:** If a student shares ideas, methods, or framework derived from this curriculum with others — in lectures, writing, or any form of teaching — they should be clear about the source and not represent Academy material as their own original synthesis.

**Accuracy:** Students should represent the tradition honestly, including its uncertainties, historical disputes, and the experimental nature of their own practical results.

**The Academy does not authorise students to teach or represent this curriculum on its behalf.** Completion of the three-year programme qualifies students within their own practice; it does not create any franchise, teaching licence, or representative status with regard to the Academy.

---

### Part Five — Termination of Academy Oversight

**The Academy's duty of care toward the student formally and fully terminates at the completion of Year Three, Month Twelve.**

Upon the student's signing and submission of the Declaration of Completion (Year Three Month 12 Week 4), the following is affirmed:

- The student's participation in the formal TTAO Inner Academy programme has concluded.
- The Academy's educational oversight, Guide relationship, and all associated support structures have concluded.
- The Academy's duty of care — to the extent any such duty existed within the educational relationship — is fully discharged.
- The student enters the post-curriculum phase as an independent practitioner, bearing full and sole responsibility for any alchemical work they undertake thereafter.

**The Academy retains no ongoing responsibility for, and disclaims all liability arising from, any alchemical work, use of preparations, teaching, or other activity the student undertakes after programme completion.**

This termination of oversight applies regardless of:
- Whether the student continues to engage with alchemical work
- Whether the student continues to use materials, methods, or frameworks learned in the curriculum
- Whether the student seeks informal contact with Guides or other Academy affiliates after completion

Post-completion contact with the Academy — whether by email, forum, or otherwise — is welcomed as collegial exchange and carries no implication of ongoing duty of care, mentorship, or therapeutic or safety oversight.

**If a student is engaged in alchemical work after programme completion and encounters a safety question or emergency, they should consult appropriately qualified professionals — not the Academy.**

---

### Part Six — Amendment and Removal

The Academy reserves the right to amend this agreement and all Safety Documents. Students will be notified of material changes.

The Academy reserves the right to remove from the programme any student who:
- Shares or transfers preparations to others
- Engages in conduct that creates risk of harm to fellow students or community members
- Misrepresents their work, identity, or qualifications within the community
- Violates any term of any Academy Safety Document
- Engages in harassment, coercion, or manipulation of any Academy participant

Removal decisions are made by the Headmaster and are final.
