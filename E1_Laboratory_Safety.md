### Fire & Heat

- **Never** leave an active heating source unattended.
- Maintain a fire extinguisher (CO₂ or dry chemical) within arm's reach of the workstation at all times.
- Do not heat sealed vessels without a pressure-relief provision.
- Use heat-resistant gloves when handling hot glassware or crucibles.
- The water bath (Balneum Mariae) is the first degree of fire and is sufficient for most Year One operations. Do not advance to higher degrees without guide confirmation of readiness.
- Keep a dedicated bucket of clean sand beside the workstation for smothering small fires in open vessels.

---

### Alcohol & Flammable Menstrua

- High-proof spirits of wine are flammable. Keep them away from open flame during use, especially while pouring.
- Store all proof spirits in sealed dark glass bottles, away from heat sources and direct sunlight.
- Do not store more than one litre of high-proof alcohol in a home workspace without a proper flameproof storage cabinet.
- The **Cotton Wick Test** (Year One, Month 3) must be passed before any alcohol is used as a menstruum. This requirement is non-negotiable.
- Legal restrictions on the distillation and possession of high-proof spirits vary by jurisdiction. Students are **solely responsible** for understanding and complying with their local laws — including Oregon Liquor and Cannabis Commission (OLCC) regulations if located in Oregon — before obtaining or producing rectified spirits of wine. **The Academy disclaims any and all liability for legal non-compliance, accidents, or damages arising from the use or storage of flammable menstrua.**

### Glassware

- Use only **borosilicate (Pyrex-type) glass** for all heating operations.
- Inspect all glassware for cracks, star fractures, chips, or thin spots before each session. Discard compromised pieces.
- Never apply sudden heat to cold glassware. Warm gradually.
- Do not set hot glassware on a cold or wet surface.
- Label every vessel in permanent marker before filling: contents, date, and tier of preparation.

### Ventilation

- **All calcination must be performed either outdoors or with direct ventilation to the outside.**
- All long digestions and distillations should be conducted in well-ventilated spaces.
- If a preparation produces an unexpected or very pungent smell, cease the operation immediately, ventilate the space, and consult your guide before resuming.

**Disclaimer:** Students are solely responsible for ensuring their workspace has adequate and safe ventilation. The academy disclaims all liability for inadequate ventilation or related health issues.

### Terminology Note — Procedural Completion

Throughout this curriculum, laboratory assessments evaluate whether a student has correctly executed a procedure according to established alchemical and chemical methods. The terms **"Procedurally Complete"** or **"Operationally Successful"** describe this evaluation. These terms indicate that the *process* was executed correctly as an educational matter. They do **not** constitute:

- Verification of the chemical purity of any preparation
- Authentication of any preparation for any consumptive use
- Endorsement or certification of any product
- Any statement about the safety of any preparation for internal use by any person

The Academy performs no product testing, assay, or quality certification of any kind. The student assumes sole responsibility for any decision regarding personal use of self-produced preparations, subject to the terms of Safety Document E.4.

### Record-Keeping as Safety Practice

Every laboratory session must be logged in the alchemical journal:
- Date and time
- Materials used (substance, source, quantity)
- Procedure performed
- Observations (including unexpected occurrences, colour changes, smells, or anomalies)
- Any safety concerns noted

### Emergency Procedures

**Fire:** Smother with the sand bucket if small and contained. Use the CO₂ extinguisher if spreading. Evacuate and call emergency services (911) if not immediately controllable.

**Alcohol spill with ignition:** Do not use water. Smother with sand or CO₂. Remove all ignition sources from the area before cleaning up unignited spills.

**Glass cut:** Clean, apply pressure, seek medical attention if deep.

**Overheating preparation:** Do not open the vessel. Remove from heat source. Allow to cool before handling.

**Persistent unusual smell:** Cease the operation, ventilate the space, and consult your guide before resuming.
