## Overview

Mineral work in Year Three introduces hazardous materials not present in vegetable or water work. The principal hazard is **antimony** — specifically stibnite (antimony trisulfide). A secondary category of hazard is corrosive solvents used in advanced Via Humida work.

These materials are genuinely hazardous when mishandled. They are manageable with appropriate precautions. The following requirements are conditions of participation.

---

### Photographic Safety Verification — Required Before Mineral Work Begins

**Because the Academy operates as a remote, online educational institution, the Headmaster cannot physically inspect student workspaces. Visual verification is therefore required before any antimony or corrosive solvent work proceeds.**

Before the Year Three Safety Quiz is administered, the student must submit:

1. **Ventilation photograph:** A clear photograph showing the ventilation arrangement — the window, fume hood, or exhaust fan setup — with the fan or opening clearly visible. If working outdoors, a photograph of the outdoor workspace.

2. **PPE photograph:** A clear photograph showing all required PPE assembled and ready: P100 respirator or N95 minimum, chemical splash goggles, nitrile gloves, acid-resistant gloves (if corrosive solvents will be used), and dedicated lab clothing.

3. **Storage photograph:** A clear photograph showing the storage location for stibnite and/or any corrosive solvents — sealed containers, clearly labelled, separated from food preparation and living areas.

4. **Spill kit photograph:** A clear photograph showing an accessible spill kit or emergency supplies: sand or cat litter for spill absorption, running water access (sink visible, or outdoor hose), and a designated waste container for hazardous material.

**The Headmaster reviews these photographs and confirms receipt before the Safety Quiz is scheduled. Submission of photographs does not constitute Academy certification of the workspace as safe. The student remains solely responsible for the actual safety of their workspace. The Academy's review is an administrative check for obvious gaps only, not a professional safety inspection.**

---

### Antimony Safety

**Stibnite (Sb₂S₃):** Raw antimony trisulfide ore. All heated work produces **antimony trioxide (Sb₂O₃)**, classified as a possible human carcinogen (Group 2B, IARC). Inhalation is the primary route of serious exposure.

**All heating of antimony ore or compounds must be performed under one of the following conditions:**

- Fully outdoors, with the student positioned upwind
- In a dedicated fume hood venting directly to the exterior
- At an open window with a powerful exhaust fan drawing air outward, student positioned between fresh air intake and the preparation

**Do not rely on smell as a safety indicator for antimony fumes.**

---

### Personal Protective Equipment — Year Three Requirements

| Situation | Minimum PPE |
| --------- | ----------- |
| Handling raw stibnite (no heat) | Nitrile gloves, safety glasses |
| Calcining antimony compounds | Nitrile gloves, chemical splash goggles, P100 half-face respirator strongly preferred (N95 absolute minimum) |
| Corrosive solvent work (Butyrum preparations, mineral acids) | Acid-resistant gloves (butyl or neoprene), chemical splash goggles, lab coat or long-sleeved dedicated clothing |
| Any antimony work involving heat | All of the above, heat-resistant outer gloves where feasible |

---

### Handling and Work Practices

- Never handle antimony materials without gloves, even cold.
- Wash hands and forearms thoroughly after every antimony session, even if gloves were worn.
- Do not eat, drink, or touch your face during or immediately after any antimony work session.
- Keep antimony work sessions to a reasonable duration. Shorter, attentive sessions are safer than long habituated ones.
- Work with the smallest quantity of material that will accomplish the preparation.
- Weigh and record all quantities in the laboratory journal before beginning any session.

---

### Storage

- Store all antimony materials in **sealed, clearly labelled containers**, separate from all other laboratory materials.
- Store away from children, animals, and household members who have not received this safety briefing.
- Store well away from food preparation and eating areas.
- Do not store antimony near strong acids or oxidising agents.
- Label containers: contents, date acquired, source, **ANTIMONY — TOXIC — DO NOT TOUCH**.

### Hazardous Materials Disposal Plan — Required Before Mineral Work Begins

**Before beginning any antimony or corrosive solvent work, each student must submit a written Hazardous Materials Disposal Plan to the Headmaster.** This plan must be submitted and acknowledged before the Safety Quiz is scheduled.

The plan must address:

1. **Your jurisdiction's disposal pathway for hazardous household chemicals:** Name the specific agency, collection site, or service you will use. In Oregon, this is typically Metro's HazardousWaste drop-off or a local household hazardous waste (HHW) event. Students outside Oregon must identify their applicable local pathway.

2. **What you will do if you discontinue the course before preparations are exhausted:** Specifically, how you will dispose of any antimony-containing materials, corrosive solvents, or contaminated equipment if you withdraw from the Academy at any point during Year Three.

3. **Interim storage:** How materials will be stored safely between sessions and between disposal opportunities.

4. **Emergency contact:** The name of one local adult who is aware you are working with hazardous materials and could assist in the event of your incapacity.

**The Hazardous Materials Disposal Plan is an administrative requirement, not a regulatory approval. The Academy reviews it to confirm that the student has identified a pathway. The student remains solely and entirely responsible for actual legal compliance with all applicable hazardous waste regulations, including Oregon Environmental Quality Commission rules (OAR Chapter 340) or the equivalent in their jurisdiction.**

---

### Corrosive Solvents — Additional Precautions

- Research every reagent fully before purchase or use.
- Never mix unknown materials.
- Have **running water immediately accessible** during all corrosive solvent work.
- **Acid on skin:** Rinse immediately with copious running water for at least 15 minutes. Seek medical attention if burning persists.
- **Acid in eyes:** Rinse immediately with running water, holding the eye open, for at least 15 minutes. Seek emergency medical attention.
- Inform your Guide before beginning any corrosive solvent work and confirm your workspace meets all requirements.

---

### Year Three Safety Quiz

Every student must pass a safety assessment covering:

1. Antimony toxicity pathways (inhalation, skin absorption, ingestion)
2. Correct ventilation setup for the student's specific workspace
3. Required PPE for calcination, handling, and corrosive work
4. Emergency responses: inhalation, skin contact, eye contact, spill, fire
5. Storage and labelling requirements
6. Disposal regulations for the student's jurisdiction
7. Contents of the student's submitted Hazardous Materials Disposal Plan

The quiz is administered by the Headmaster or a designated Guide after photographs and the Disposal Plan have been received and acknowledged. **Mineral work does not begin until the quiz is passed.**

---

### Disposal

Antimony waste — spent ore, calcined ash, used solutions, contaminated materials — must be disposed of as **hazardous waste**. Do not pour antimony-containing liquids down the drain. Do not dispose of solid antimony waste in ordinary household rubbish.

**Students are solely and fully responsible for understanding and complying with all applicable laws and regulations concerning the disposal of hazardous chemical waste in their jurisdiction. The Academy disclaims all liability for any environmental damage, legal penalties, or other consequences arising from improper disposal.**
