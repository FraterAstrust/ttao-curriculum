# TTAO Inner Academy

## Guide Code of Conduct & Educational Facilitator Agreement

---

# IMPORTANT NOTICE

**This document governs the role, scope, conduct, and limitations of all Guides affiliated with the TTAO Inner Academy. It must be read, understood, and signed before any Guide undertakes any facilitation role within this programme.**

**TTAO Inner Academy is an educational institution. Guides serve in an educational facilitation capacity only. No Guide role creates or implies a therapeutic, medical, pastoral, mentoring, legal, financial, or personal advisory relationship with any student.**

**Guides are not employees of TTAO Inner Academy unless explicitly engaged under a separate written employment agreement. This document governs the scope and conduct of facilitation; it does not constitute an employment contract or create an ongoing engagement.**

**All actions taken in the Guide role are governed by the laws of the State of Oregon.**

---

### Part One — Definition of the Guide Role

A Guide within the TTAO Inner Academy is an **Educational Facilitator**. The Guide has completed the three-year TTAO Inner Academy curriculum in good standing and has been invited by the Headmaster to support students progressing through Years Two and Three.

The Guide's function is limited to:

1. **Curriculum support:** Helping assigned students understand and engage with the curriculum content — readings, laboratory methods, inner work practices, and symbolic frameworks taught in the programme.

2. **Administrative coordination:** Scheduling and participating in quarterly check-in sessions, reviewing student journal submissions for curriculum completeness, coordinating the Year Three Safety Quiz and photographic verification process.

3. **Progress facilitation:** Identifying when a student's progress has stalled, documenting this observation, and consulting the Headmaster about appropriate next steps.

4. **Signposting:** When a student raises concerns that fall outside the scope of the curriculum — medical, psychological, legal, relational, or financial — the Guide clearly states that the matter falls outside their role and directs the student to appropriate professional resources.

5. **Emergency signposting:** When a student discloses or displays signs of crisis (see Safety Document E.2), the Guide responds according to the Crisis-Response SOP: providing crisis line information, notifying the Headmaster, and declining to provide any form of crisis counselling or risk assessment.

---

### Part Two — Scope Limitations — What Guides Do Not Do

The following are explicitly outside the Guide role. A Guide who is asked to engage in any of the following must decline clearly and redirect.

**Guides do not:**

- Provide medical advice, health information, or recommendations regarding any physical symptom, illness, medication, treatment, or preparation.

- Assess or advise on any student's psychological health, mental state, emotional wellbeing, or suitability for therapeutic intervention.

- Provide crisis counselling, suicide risk assessment, safety planning, or any form of acute psychological support. In all crisis situations, the Guide's sole function is to provide the relevant crisis resource and notify the Headmaster.

- Offer legal advice of any kind, including regarding the legality of any preparation, chemical, or alchemical practice in the student's jurisdiction.

- Offer financial advice of any kind.

- Serve as a personal mentor, life coach, sponsor, or spiritual director.

- Accept, handle, receive, or facilitate the transfer of any preparation, material, or substance from any student. If a student offers to share a preparation, the Guide declines immediately and reminds the student of the absolute prohibition in Safety Document E.4.

- Recommend that a student use, continue, or discontinue any preparation they have produced.

- Make representations about the efficacy, safety, or quality of any preparation or method described in the curriculum.

- Represent themselves as an authority on the alchemical tradition beyond the scope of the curriculum they have completed.

- Provide instruction, guidance, or advice on alchemical work outside the scope of the TTAO three-year curriculum.

- Enter into financial transactions of any kind with students — including but not limited to receiving payment for additional sessions, guidance, materials, or advice.

---

### Part Three — Conduct Standards

**Honesty:** Guides represent their own experience and knowledge accurately. A Guide who has not personally encountered a difficulty a student raises says so, and either consults the Headmaster or directs the student to the relevant curriculum resource.

**Confidentiality:** Information disclosed by students in Guide sessions is held in confidence within the Academy's administrative team (Guide and Headmaster). It is not shared with other students, with external parties, or used for any purpose other than supporting the student's curriculum progress, except where disclosure is legally required or where the student's safety requires notification to the Headmaster or emergency services.

**Appropriate boundaries:** Guides maintain professional boundaries at all times. Guide sessions are not social occasions. Personal disclosures by either party that fall outside the curriculum's scope are redirected appropriately. Guides do not develop personal relationships with assigned students beyond the Guide facilitation role during the period of assignment.

**Non-exploitation:** Guides do not use the Guide role to establish personal influence over students, to promote their own alchemical practice, writing, or teaching, or to recruit students into any other programme, service, or relationship.

**Consistency:** Guides apply Academy policies — including all Safety Documents — consistently, without exception, and without granting informal exemptions. No Guide has authority to waive or modify any requirement in any Safety Document.

---

### Part Four — Required Disclosures

A Guide must notify the Headmaster immediately and in writing if:

- A student discloses or displays signs consistent with imminent risk to their life or the life of another person.
- A student discloses that they have shared a preparation with another person.
- A student discloses or describes a laboratory incident involving injury, chemical exposure, or emergency response.
- A Guide becomes aware that a student is representing Academy material publicly in a manner that is materially inaccurate or that creates reputational or legal risk for the Academy.
- A Guide experiences any situation in a student session that causes them concern and for which they do not have clear guidance in this document or the Safety Documents.

**When in doubt, the Guide notifies the Headmaster.** The Guide does not attempt to manage ambiguous or difficult situations independently.

---

### Part Five — Guide Wellbeing and Limits

The Guide role is voluntary and part-time. Guides are not expected to be available outside scheduled sessions or during personal emergencies. If a Guide receives a distressing student communication at an unexpected time, the Guide:

1. Provides the relevant crisis resource if the communication suggests risk.
2. Notifies the Headmaster as soon as practical.
3. Does not engage in extended out-of-session correspondence regarding the student's distress.

A Guide who finds the role distressing, who feels a student is making demands beyond the role's scope, or who is uncertain how to handle a situation contacts the Headmaster for support. The Headmaster may reassign students or adjust the facilitation arrangement as needed.

---

### Part Six — Termination of the Guide Role

The Guide role terminates:

- When the assigned student completes Year Three Month Twelve.
- When a student is administratively removed from the programme.
- When a Guide resigns from the role by notifying the Headmaster.
- When the Headmaster ends the Guide arrangement for any reason.

Upon termination of the Guide role, the Guide's confidentiality obligations regarding that student's disclosures continue indefinitely.

Upon termination of the Guide role, the Guide has no further obligation to the student and no ongoing responsibility for the student's alchemical work or wellbeing.

---

### Part Seven — Liability

Guides act within the scope defined by this document. The Academy does not indemnify Guides against liability arising from actions taken outside this scope. Guides who provide advice, services, or assistance outside the Educational Facilitator role as defined here do so as private individuals, not as Academy representatives, and bear sole personal responsibility for any consequences.

Guides who act within the scope defined by this document and in accordance with all Academy Safety Documents and the Crisis-Response SOP do so as representatives of an educational institution providing curriculum support. Nothing in this document creates or implies professional therapeutic, medical, or legal status, responsibility, or liability.

---

### Acknowledgement

By signing this agreement, the Guide confirms:

- They have read, understood, and agree to abide by this Code of Conduct in full.
- They have read, understood, and can apply all Academy Safety Documents (E.1 through E.6) and the Crisis-Response SOP.
- They understand that the Guide role is an educational facilitation role only and does not create any therapeutic, medical, pastoral, or personal advisory relationship with students.
- They understand that violations of this agreement may result in immediate termination of the Guide role and may carry personal legal consequences if the violation causes harm to a student.
